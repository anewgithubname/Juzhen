Zero-Flow: vt=0.5 ≡ 0 ⟺ psource = ptarget

A rectified flow is trained (right here in your browser, via WebAssembly — no server, works offline) with independent coupling between the two distributions you choose; particles are then transported from t=0 to t=1 along the learned field. Theorem 3.1 of Zero-Flow Encoders (Wang, Wang, Liu & Suzuki, ICML 2026): the velocity field vanishes everywhere at t = 0.5 if and only if source = target. Pick the same dataset twice and watch everything freeze blue at the midpoint.

Everything runs locally: a 3-layer MLP v(x,t) is trained with Adam on E‖X′ − X − u(Xt, t)‖², Xt = tX′ + (1−t)X, X ⟂ X′, then dX = v dt is integrated. Antisymmetry (Thm 3.2): with identical distributions the field reverses around t = 0.5 — particles drift, stop dead at the midpoint, then flow back out. Single-file demo built with Emscripten.
