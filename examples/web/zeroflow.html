The Zero-Flow Phenomenon

\(v_{1/2} \equiv 0 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; p_{\mathrm{source}} = p_{\mathrm{target}}\) — Theorem 3.1 of Zero-Flow Encoders, Wang, Wang, Liu & Suzuki, ICML 2026

A rectified flow is trained with independent coupling between the two distributions you choose; particles are then transported from \(t = 0\) to \(t = 1\) along the learned velocity field. The field vanishes everywhere at \(t = \tfrac{1}{2}\) if and only if the source and target distributions coincide. Pick the same dataset twice and watch everything freeze blue at the midpoint.

Implementation. The Juzhen (C++/CUDA) backend trains a velocity-field MLP \(u(x, t)\) with Adam on $$\min_{u}\;\mathbb{E}\,\bigl\|\,X' - X - u(X_t,\, t)\,\bigr\|^{2}, \qquad X_t = t\,X' + (1 - t)\,X,\quad X \perp X',$$ then integrates \(\mathrm{d}X = u\,\mathrm{d}t\) from \(t = 0\) to \(1\) and streams the animation to this page. Antisymmetry (Theorem 3.2): with identical distributions the field satisfies \(v_t = -v_{1-t}\) — particles drift, stop dead at the midpoint, then flow back out.
