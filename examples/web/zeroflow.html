A rectified flow is trained with independent coupling between the two distributions you choose, then particles are transported from t=0 to t=1 along the learned field. Theorem 3.1 of Zero-Flow Encoders (Wang, Wang, Liu & Suzuki, ICML 2026): the velocity field vanishes everywhere at t = 0.5 if and only if source = target.
